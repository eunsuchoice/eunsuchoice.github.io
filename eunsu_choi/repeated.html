REPEATED: a short musical

Over time, the stories of Korean 'comfort women' may have become the stories of “grandmothers” to us. However, these “grandmothers” were once girls and women like us before. REPEATED: a short musical was created to show the resonances across generations in regard to the lived experiences of women.

Sojeong is a character from the past who leaves her home to support her family during the Japanese occupation. Jeongeun is a character from our modern time who is about to start her career. Two characters who are seemingly different, share similar stories. “Where do you see yourself in 10 years?” Will they be able to realize their dreams despite their harsh realities?

Who has made changes in this world, and who will make changes from now on? If something keeps repeating though we know it is wrong, when will it come to an end, and by whom?

We wish this work will help our audience tackle these difficult questions.

Currently in South Korea, there remains 9 known survivors of Korean 'comfort women'.
